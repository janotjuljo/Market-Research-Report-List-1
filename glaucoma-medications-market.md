<p><ul><li>Prostaglandin Analogs</li><li>Beta Blockers</li><li>Alpha Agonists</li><li>Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors</li><li>Combined Medications</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Glaucoma medications are essential in managing intraocular pressure to prevent vision loss. Prostaglandin analogs enhance the drainage of fluid from the eye. Beta blockers reduce fluid production. Alpha agonists both decrease fluid production and increase drainage. Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors lower fluid secretion within the eye. Combined medications offer the benefits of two or more drug classes, improving patient compliance and effectiveness. Each type plays a pivotal role in a comprehensive glaucoma treatment plan, tailored to individual patient needs.</p></p>
