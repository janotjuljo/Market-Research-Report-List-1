<p><p>The influenza vaccines market predominantly features trivalent and quadrivalent flu vaccines. Trivalent flu vaccines protect against three virus strains: two A viruses and one B virus, primarily targeting the most common circulating strains. In contrast, quadrivalent flu vaccines offer broader protection by including an additional B virus strain, enhancing efficacy against more circulating viruses. This expanded coverage aims to reduce the incidence of influenza in various populations, making quadrivalent vaccines increasingly popular among healthcare providers and consumers.</p></p>
