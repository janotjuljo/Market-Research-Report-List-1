<p><p>Gas distribution systems can be categorized into distributed and integrated markets. In a distributed market, natural gas is supplied from various smaller sources, allowing for localized production and consumption, enhancing resilience and efficiency. Conversely, an integrated market features a centralized approach, where gas is sourced from large production facilities and delivered through a unified pipeline network. This model promotes economies of scale but may reduce flexibility in response to localized demand changes. Both systems cater to distinct energy requirements and geographical challenges.</p></p>
