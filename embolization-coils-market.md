<p><p>The embolization coils market consists of two primary types: detachable and pushable coils. Detachable coils are designed to allow precise placement and removal during procedures, making them ideal for complex cases. Conversely, pushable coils are advanced through a catheter and deployed in a more straightforward manner, often used for simpler embolization tasks. Both types serve critical roles in minimally invasive treatments for conditions like vascular malformations and aneurysms, improving patient outcomes and procedural efficiency.</p></p>
